Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for your programs?

For undergraduate programs (B.Sc), candidates must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with science stream (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics) with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks. For MBA program, candidates must have a bachelor's degree in any discipline with minimum 50% marks and preferably work experience in healthcare sector.

What is the fee structure for various programs?

The fee ranges from ₹75,000 to ₹1,50,000 per year for undergraduate programs and approximately ₹2,50,000 per year for the MBA program. Exact fees vary by program and centre. We offer scholarships based on merit and financial need. For detailed fee structure, please contact our admissions office.

Are there any scholarships or financial assistance available?

Yes, we offer merit-based scholarships ranging from 10% to 50% of the tuition fee. Additionally, we have need-based financial assistance programs. Students can also avail educational loans from our partner banks with special interest rates. A separate scholarship application needs to be submitted along with supporting documents.

What are the career prospects after completing these programs?

Our allied health graduates enjoy excellent employment opportunities in hospitals, diagnostic centers, research institutions, healthcare companies, and educational institutions. Many of our graduates are directly absorbed into Apollo Hospitals network. We have a dedicated placement cell that helps students with career guidance, interview preparation, and job placements. Our placement rate is consistently above 90%.

Do you offer hostel facilities for students?

Yes, we provide safe and comfortable hostel accommodations at all our centres. The hostels are equipped with modern amenities, 24/7 security, and mess facilities. Hostel fees range from ₹75,000 to ₹1,00,000 per year, including accommodation and meals. Limited seats are available, so early application is recommended.

Is there an entrance exam for admission?

Yes, we conduct a standardized Aptitude and Knowledge Assessment Test (AKAT) for all applicants. The test covers basic sciences, general knowledge, logical reasoning, and English. For the MBA program, we also accept CAT/MAT/XAT scores. Shortlisted candidates are called for a personal interview before final selection.

What is the academic calendar for the upcoming batch?

Our academic year typically begins in July/August. The application process starts in January, with entrance exams conducted in April/May and admission offers sent out by June. Each academic year consists of two semesters, with semester breaks in December and May.

Do you provide practical training and internships?

Absolutely! Practical training is an integral part of our curriculum. Students undergo regular clinical rotations at Apollo Hospitals and partner healthcare facilities. Final year includes a mandatory internship of 6 months. We also facilitate international internship opportunities for deserving students through our global healthcare partnerships.
